Balanced Eating Without Deprivation

Casey Dennis

Published on January 15, 2025

Learn how to enjoy all foods in moderation while maintaining a healthy, balanced diet that fuels your body and soul.

The Myth of "Good" and "Bad" Foods

One of the biggest misconceptions in nutrition is the idea that foods are either "good" or "bad." This black-and-white thinking often leads to guilt, shame, and an unhealthy relationship with food. The truth is, all foods can fit into a balanced diet when consumed mindfully and in appropriate portions.

The 80/20 Approach

I love teaching the 80/20 approach to eating. This means that 80% of your food choices should be nutrient-dense, whole foods that nourish your body, while 20% can be foods you enjoy purely for pleasure. This approach allows for flexibility while still prioritizing your health.

What does the 80% look like?

  • Lean proteins (chicken, fish, tofu, legumes)
  • Colorful vegetables and fruits
  • Whole grains (quinoa, brown rice, oats)
  • Healthy fats (avocado, nuts, olive oil)
  • Plenty of water

And the 20%?

This is where you can enjoy that piece of chocolate, a glass of wine with dinner, or your favorite comfort food. The key is savoring these foods mindfully rather than eating them out of guilt or restriction.

Practical Tips for Balanced Eating

1. Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to hunger and fullness cues. Eat when you're hungry, stop when you're satisfied. This takes practice, especially if you've been dieting for years, but it's one of the most important skills you can develop.

2. Plan Your Indulgences

Instead of randomly eating treats throughout the week, plan for them. If you know you want dessert after dinner, make sure the rest of your day is filled with nutritious choices. This way, you can enjoy your treat without guilt.

3. Focus on Addition, Not Subtraction

Instead of focusing on what you can't eat, focus on adding more nutritious foods to your diet. Try to include a vegetable with every meal, add a piece of fruit to your snack, or drink an extra glass of water.

4. Practice Mindful Eating

Slow down and pay attention to your food. Put away distractions, chew slowly, and really taste what you're eating. This helps with digestion and allows you to truly enjoy your food.

Sample Day of Balanced Eating

Breakfast:

Greek yogurt with berries, granola, and a drizzle of honey

Lunch:

Quinoa bowl with roasted vegetables, chickpeas, and tahini dressing

Snack:

Apple slices with almond butter

Dinner:

Grilled salmon with sweet potato and steamed broccoli

Evening Treat:

A small piece of dark chocolate with herbal tea

Remember: Progress, Not Perfection

The goal isn't to eat perfectly all the time. It's to develop a healthy, sustainable relationship with food that allows you to nourish your body while still enjoying life's pleasures. Some days you'll eat more treats, some days you'll eat more vegetables, and that's perfectly normal.

Start small, be patient with yourself, and remember that every small step towards balanced eating is a victory worth celebrating.
