Building a Self-Care Routine That Sticks

Casey Dennis

Published on February 5, 2025

Create sustainable self-care habits that fit your lifestyle and help you prioritize your mental and physical well-being.

Self-Care Isn't Selfish—It's Essential

Let's clear something up right away: taking care of yourself isn't a luxury, and it's definitely not selfish. Self-care is about maintaining your physical, mental, and emotional health so you can show up as your best self for everyone and everything that matters to you.

Think of it like the airplane safety instructions—you put on your own oxygen mask first before helping others. You can't pour from an empty cup, and sustainable self-care helps keep your cup full.

What Self-Care Really Looks Like

Forget the Instagram-perfect bubble baths and expensive spa days (though those can be nice too!). Real self-care is often much simpler and more practical than social media makes it seem.

Physical Self-Care

  • Getting enough sleep (7-9 hours for most adults)
  • Eating nourishing foods regularly
  • Moving your body in ways that feel good
  • Staying hydrated
  • Taking breaks when you need them
  • Going to medical appointments

Mental & Emotional Self-Care

  • Setting healthy boundaries
  • Practicing mindfulness or meditation
  • Journaling or processing emotions
  • Saying no to commitments that drain you
  • Seeking therapy or counseling when needed
  • Engaging in hobbies you enjoy

Social Self-Care

  • Spending time with people who lift you up
  • Limiting time with energy vampires
  • Asking for help when you need it
  • Maintaining meaningful relationships
  • Setting boundaries with social media

Building Your Personal Self-Care Toolkit

Self-care isn't one-size-fits-all. What works for your best friend might not work for you, and that's perfectly okay. The key is finding what genuinely makes you feel restored and energized.

Quick Self-Care Ideas (5-15 minutes)

  • Take 10 deep breaths
  • Step outside for fresh air
  • Listen to your favorite song
  • Do some gentle stretches
  • Write down three things you're grateful for
  • Make a cup of tea mindfully
  • Call someone who makes you laugh
  • Take a hot shower

Longer Self-Care Activities (30+ minutes)

  • Take a walk in nature
  • Read a book for pleasure
  • Try a new recipe
  • Practice yoga or meditation
  • Work on a creative project
  • Take a relaxing bath
  • Have a phone-free evening
  • Organize a space that's been bothering you

Creating Your Daily Self-Care Routine

Start Small and Be Consistent

The biggest mistake people make with self-care is trying to do too much at once. Instead of overhauling your entire life, start with one small habit and build from there.

Sample Daily Self-Care Routine

Morning (5 minutes):

Before checking your phone, take 5 deep breaths and set an intention for the day

Midday (10 minutes):

Step away from your desk, go outside, or do some gentle stretches

Evening (15 minutes):

Wind down with a calming activity—reading, journaling, or a skincare routine

Overcoming Common Self-Care Obstacles

"I Don't Have Time"

Self-care doesn't have to take hours. Even 2-3 minutes of deep breathing can make a difference. Look for small pockets of time throughout your day, and remember that taking care of yourself actually gives you more energy and focus for everything else.

"I Feel Guilty"

This is especially common for caregivers and people-pleasers. Remember that taking care of yourself allows you to better care for others. You're not being selfish—you're being responsible.

"I Don't Know What I Need"

Start by paying attention to how different activities make you feel. Do you feel energized after a walk? Calmer after journaling? More connected after calling a friend? Use these clues to build your personal self-care menu.

Self-Care for Different Seasons of Life

When You're Overwhelmed

Focus on the basics: sleep, nutrition, and asking for help. This isn't the time for elaborate self-care routines—it's time for survival mode self-care.

When You're Busy but Managing

Integrate self-care into existing routines. Listen to a podcast you love during your commute, do stretches while watching TV, or practice gratitude while brushing your teeth.

When You Have More Time

This is when you can explore longer self-care activities and maybe even plan some special treats for yourself. But don't abandon your daily basics—they're what keep you steady.

Making Self-Care a Non-Negotiable

Weekly Self-Care Check-In Questions:

  • How am I feeling physically, mentally, and emotionally?
  • What's been draining my energy this week?
  • What's been filling my cup?
  • What do I need more of next week?
  • What do I need less of?

Remember: Progress, Not Perfection

Some weeks you'll nail your self-care routine, and some weeks you'll barely manage the basics. Both are okay. Self-care isn't about being perfect—it's about being intentional about taking care of yourself, even in small ways.

Start where you are, with what you have, for however long you can manage. Your future self will thank you for every small act of self-care, no matter how simple it seems.

You deserve to feel good in your own life. You deserve to prioritize your well-being. And you definitely deserve to take up space and care for yourself with the same compassion you show others.
